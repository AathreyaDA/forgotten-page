Fragments of Yesterday

In silent corners of forgotten towns, stories linger like dust in sunlight.

Some are carried by the wind, whispered between trees that remember too much.

Fragments remain in places people stopped visiting, yet never truly left.

Carefully written letters fade, but their echoes refuse to disappear.

Records of moments lost still exist in patterns only the patient can see.

Stories of yesterday are never gone, only waiting to be read differently.
